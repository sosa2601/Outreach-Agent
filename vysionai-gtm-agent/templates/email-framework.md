## Voice and Tone Rules

### Always
- Write like a real person — direct, specific, no fluff
- Reference something real about them in Email 1
- Use simple words — no jargon, no buzzwords
- Sound like you did 10 minutes of research, not zero and not 2 hours

### Never
- "Hope this finds you well"
- "I wanted to reach out"
- "I came across your profile"
- "We help companies like yours"
- "Would love to connect"
- "Let me know your thoughts"
- "Feel free to..."
- Lists of features or benefits
- More than one ask per email
- More than 80 words in Email 1

## Email 1 — Structure

```
[Anchor line — specific to what you found about them]
[One sentence connecting anchor to the GTM gap]
[One outcome statement — what Saswati delivers]
[Yes/no CTA question]
```

**Length:** 60–80 words
**CTA format:** A simple yes/no question. Not "let's chat." Not "book a call."

**Example CTA questions:**
- "Is your outbound sequence fully automated right now?"
- "Worth a 10-minute look at how it would work for [Company]?"
- "Would it make sense to show you the exact system?"
- "Is building a signal-based outbound layer on your roadmap for Q3?"

## Email 3 — Structure

```

[Leave the door open — no pressure]

```

**Length:** 30–40 words
**Tone:** Warm. Not passive-aggressive. Not guilt-tripping.

**Example closes:**

- "Not the right time? No problem — happy to reconnect when the timing makes more sense."
- "Closing the loop here — let me know if this comes up later."

## Subject Line Rules

- Maximum 6 words
- No clickbait, no false urgency
- No question marks in the subject (sounds like spam)
- Lowercase preferred (less formal, higher open rates)
- Should hint at the anchor or the problem — not the solution

**Good examples:**
- "outbound without the ops overhead"
- "signal-based GTM for [Company]"
- "the system behind your SDR team"
- "automated outreach — worth 10 min?"
- "RevOps without the RevOps hire"

**Bad examples:**
- "Quick question for you!"
- "Are you struggling with outbound?"
- "I can help you 10x your pipeline"
- "Following up"

## Anchor Quality Guide

When email-writer uses an anchor, it must meet this bar:

### Strong Anchor (use immediately)
- They wrote a LinkedIn post in last 30 days about the exact problem Saswati solves
- They posted a job description for a RevOps or GTM role (hiring signal)
- Their company just raised funding and they announced it on LinkedIn
- They spoke at an event or podcast about GTM challenges

**Example first line:**
"Saw your post last week about building outbound from scratch at [Company] —
the part about not having a RevOps layer yet hit home."

### Medium Anchor (use with care)
- Recent company news (product launch, partnership, expansion)
- Career milestone (new role in last 6 months, promotion)
- General GTM content they published

**Example first line:**
"Noticed [Company] just launched [product] — usually signals the next push
is building the outbound layer to support it."

### Weak Anchor (flag for human review)
- "You work in B2B SaaS"
- "I noticed you're the Head of Growth"
- Generic industry content with no specificity

**If only weak anchor available:** Flag the sequence. Do not send without human review.
